One-liner

PrimePop is the math game that feels like bubble wrap: pop pairs of bubbles that add or subtract into prime numbers, earn stars, and chase perfect times.

Description

Bubbles float up the screen, each holding a number. Tap two whose sum — or difference — is a prime number, and they pop. Hit the level's target score before you run out of lives; finish fast to earn three stars. That's the entire rule set, taught by a one-minute interactive tutorial, and it turns out to be a quietly devious brain workout: players do dozens of quick sums a round without noticing.

PrimePop was built by a solo developer as a love letter to two things that don't usually meet: the tactile joy of bubble wrap and the elegance of prime numbers. It ships with three hand-tuned visual themes — sunny Bubble Lagoon, purple Aurora, and midnight Starlight — and collects no personal data: progress lives entirely on the player's device.
